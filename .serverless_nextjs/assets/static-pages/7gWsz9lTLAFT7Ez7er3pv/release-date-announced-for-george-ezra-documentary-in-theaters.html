    Release Date Announced for George Ezra Documentary in Theaters

    A new documentary following British singer-songwriter George Ezra has confirmed for release in UK cinemas on 29 August. The documentary follows the release of the 29-year-old’s third studio album, Gold Rush Kid.

    From Lands End in England to the UK mainland

    The film, titled End to End, follows the journey of Ezra in 2021. It covers a distance of 1,200 miles in 95 days from Lands End in southern England to John O’Groats, the northernmost tip of the UK mainland.

    Read Also:-Tanya Burr is Pregnant YouTube Star Reveals She Expecting a Baby With a Secret Partner

    Your relationship with your music and live performances

    Meeting musicians along the way. He searches the country and reunites after months of separation. while reflecting on his relationship with his music and live performances. The documentary’s London launch event will include an intimate live performance by Ezra that will broadcast in cinemas across the country.

    Speaking about the documentary, Ezra said: “I started running right after I finished the album. Mixing started. on which I was working. The tour was therefore sound-track by the new album and each made the other more meaningful.

    changed my perception of the world

    “It wasn’t lost on me that this was a real life-changing moment, it really changed my perception of the world.” I am so glad that audiences will be able to see it on the big screen and we can celebrate the experience. “

    End to End will feature acoustic sets and tracks from Gold Rush Kid and will provide an insight into Ezra’s process of bringing his third album to life.

    Speaking before the release of the documentary

    Speaking ahead of the release of the documentary. Producer Richard Yee said: After being deprived of live music during the lockdown, it was a pleasure to bring George’s intimate performances and life-affirming vision to the screen. Because he had trekked the length of the UK with his two. closest friends.

    “With George’s unparalleled reach throughout the journey, End to End is a gripping, moving, and uplifting film that is unlike any music documentary you’ve seen before.”
